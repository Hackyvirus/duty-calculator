Background

Export oriented unit scheme (EOU) or Export Promotion Capital goods (EPCG) + advance authorisation (AA) or Drawback Scheme allow import of capital goods, raw materials, consumables without payment of duties of customs in terms of respective customs exemption notifications. The EOU scheme allows import of even the used capital goods.

All the schemes mandate fulfilment of export obligation unlike in case of MOOWR. The schemes have a mechanism to neutralize the duties of customs involved even in domestically procured goods as the inward supplies received by the EOU, AA holder, EPCG holder is treated as deemed export. In case of EOUs when they sale the imported capital goods in DTA they are entitled to claim depreciation in the transaction value at the time of import for the purpose of payment of applicable duties of customs provided they have fulfilled the export obligation. In case of EPCG the no duties are payable on the capital goods on DTA sale once the export obligation is discharged.

Further, units having substantial raw material imports can accrue savings in working capital costs by way of deferment of Customs duties on imports.

Further, EOU, AA & EPCG holder are entitled to export incentives viz. Remission of duties & taxes on export products (RoDTEP) on export of the goods eligible for such benefit. However, they are not entitled for the All Industry Rate of duty drawback (‘AIR’). Operating as a regular unit availing AIR is more beneficial when majority of the raw materials used in export goods are domestically sourced.
